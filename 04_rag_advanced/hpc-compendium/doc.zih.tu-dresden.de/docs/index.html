ZIH HPC Documentation#

This is the documentation of the HPC systems and services provided at TU Dresden/ZIH. Resources are funded by Nationales Hochleistungsrechnen (NHR) and the Free State of Saxony.

This documentation will be continuously updated, since we try to incorporate more information with increasing experience and with every question you ask us.

If the provided HPC systems and services helped to advance your research, please cite us. Why this is important and acknowledgment examples can be found in the section Acknowledgement.

Support and Consultation#

We offer regular public and personal consultation opportunities with HPC experts and NHR specialist consultants for life and earth system sciences.

Training and Courses#

We offer a rich and colorful bouquet of courses from classical HPC introduction (HPC introduction slides) to various Performance Analysis and Machine Learning trainings. Please refer to the page Training Offers for a detailed overview of the courses and the respective dates at ZIH.

The recorded presentation of the HPC introduction course is divided by topic and available for viewing:

  • Linux basics

  • Access the HPC system

  • Cluster overview

  • Filesystems

  • Workspaces and data transfer

  • Batch system

  • Job Monitoring with PIKA

  • Software

  • Project Management

  • Application for Resources

  • Software development tools

  • Support

Furthermore, Center for Scalable Data Analytics and Artificial Intelligence ScaDS.AI Dresden/Leipzig offers various training opportunities with HPC focus. Current schedule and registration is available at the ScaDS.AI trainings page.

TU Dresden is one of the NHR centres and part of the Gauss Alliance. Trainings offered by these organisations can be found in the HPC Calendar.

Contribution#

The HPC team invites you to take part in the improvement of these pages by correcting or adding useful information. Your contributions are highly welcome!

The easiest way for you to contribute is to report issues via the GitLab issue tracking system. Please check for any already existing issue before submitting your issue in order to avoid duplicate issues.

Please also find out the other ways you could contribute in our guidelines how to contribute.

!!! tip “Reminder”

Non-documentation issues and requests need to be send to
[hpc-support@tu-dresden.de](mailto:hpc-support@tu-dresden.de).
