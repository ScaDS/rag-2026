Historical Overview#

Year

System

1968

Zeiss-Rechenautomat Nr. 1 (ZRA1) Performance: 150 to 200 instructions/s, 4096 storage cells with 48 bit each (magnetic drum)

1970

Commissioning of large stand-alone computers, coupled stand-alone computers and terminals (BESM, ESER)

1976

Computer network DELTA and graphic workstations

1981

Deployment of the first microcomputers; experimental testing of local area networks (LAN)

1986

Workstation computers replace mechanical devices; the first PC pools for teaching and studying are set up

1991

Short-term operation of used mainframe computers

1993

VP200-EX (857 MFlop/s Peak)

1996

Development of infrastructure on the TU campus

1997

SGI Origin2000 (21,8 GFlop/s, 56 CPUs, 17 GB RAM, 350 GB disk capacity)

1998

Cray T3E (38,4 GFlop/s, 64 CPUs, 8 GB RAM, 100 GB disk capacity)

2001/02

SGI Origin3800 (51,2 + 102,4 GFlop/s, 64 + 128 CPUs, 64 + 64 GB RAM, 790 GB disk capacity)

Itanium-Cluster Castillo
